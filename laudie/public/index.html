Frequently Asked Questions

What should I pack for a safari?

We recommend lightweight, neutral-colored clothing, a sun hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, insect repellent, comfortable walking shoes, a light jacket or sweater for evenings, camera equipment, and personal medications. A detailed packing list will be provided upon booking.

Are there any hidden costs?

No. Our safari packages include all costs mentioned in the itinerary. Additional costs may include gratuities, alcoholic beverages, travel insurance, and personal purchases during your trip.

Is Kenya safe for tourists?

Kenya is generally safe for tourists, especially in established safari destinations. Our guides are experienced professionals who prioritize your safety. We recommend following standard travel precautions and adhering to the guidance of your safari guide.

When is the best time to visit Kenya for safari?

The dry seasons (January-February and June-October) offer the best wildlife viewing as animals gather around water sources. The Great Migration in Maasai Mara typically occurs between July and October. However, Kenya is a year-round destination with each season offering unique experiences.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept credit cards, bank transfers, and PayPal. For security reasons, we do not accept cash payments for package bookings.

Safari Planning Guide

Best Times to Visit Kenya

Dry Season (June to October)

Benefits: Best wildlife viewing, Great Migration in Maasai Mara (July-October), less rain, easier to spot animals at water sources

Considerations: Peak tourist season, higher prices, advance booking recommended

Short Dry Season (January to February)

Benefits: Good wildlife viewing, fewer tourists than peak season, newborn animals in many parks

Considerations: Can be very hot, particularly in February

Rainy Seasons (March to May & November to December)

Benefits: Lower prices, fewer tourists, lush landscapes, excellent bird watching, clear air for photography

Considerations: Some roads may be impassable, rain can interrupt game drives, some camps close during heavy rains

Safari Packing Essentials

Clothing

  • Lightweight, neutral-colored shirts (avoid bright colors)
  • Lightweight pants/convertible pants
  • Safari shorts (not too short)
  • Light jacket or fleece for cool mornings/evenings
  • Rain jacket or poncho (seasonal)
  • Wide-brimmed hat for sun protection
  • Comfortable closed walking shoes
  • Sandals for camp/lodge
  • Swimwear (if your accommodation has a pool)
  • Pajamas (lightweight)
  • Undergarments (enough for your stay)
  • Light scarf (for dust and sun protection)

Photography

  • Camera with zoom lens
  • Extra memory cards
  • Extra camera batteries
  • Battery charger
  • Lens cleaning kit
  • Binoculars
  • Camera bag with dust protection
  • Power bank

Health & Toiletries

  • Sunscreen (SPF 30+ recommended)
  • Insect repellent with DEET
  • Personal medications
  • Basic first aid kit
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Wet wipes
  • Toiletries (shampoo, soap, toothpaste)
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • Eyedrops (for dust)

Documents & Money

  • Passport with at least 6 months validity
  • Copy of passport (separate from original)
  • Travel insurance details
  • Visa documentation (if applicable)
  • Cash (USD widely accepted)
  • Credit/debit cards
  • Safari vouchers/confirmations
  • Emergency contact information
  • Yellow fever certificate (if required)

Pro Packing Tips

  • Pack light and use soft-sided luggage (easier to store in safari vehicles)
  • Many lodges offer same-day laundry service
  • Dress in layers - mornings can be cool, afternoons hot
  • Don't forget a good quality flashlight or headlamp
  • Keep electronics in dust-proof bags when not in use
  • Leave valuable jewelry at home

Health & Safety Information

Recommended Vaccinations

Consult your doctor at least 4-6 weeks before your trip to discuss vaccinations and medications. Generally recommended for Kenya:

  • Yellow Fever: Required for entry if arriving from a country with yellow fever risk
  • Hepatitis A & B
  • Typhoid
  • Tetanus-diphtheria
  • Measles-mumps-rubella (MMR)
  • Malaria prophylaxis - discuss options with your doctor

Malaria Prevention

Kenya is a malaria-risk country. Take these precautions:

  • Take appropriate anti-malarial medication (consult your doctor)
  • Use insect repellent containing DEET
  • Wear long sleeves and pants in the evening
  • Sleep under a mosquito net or in air-conditioned rooms
  • Use permethrin-treated clothing if possible

Water & Food Safety

  • Drink only bottled water (provided on all our safaris)
  • Avoid ice in drinks unless you're sure it's made from purified water
  • Eat thoroughly cooked food served hot
  • Avoid raw vegetables and fruits unless they can be peeled
  • All meals at our partnered lodges/camps follow strict hygiene protocols

Travel Insurance

We strongly recommend comprehensive travel insurance that includes:

  • Medical coverage and evacuation
  • Trip cancellation/interruption
  • Lost luggage coverage
  • 24/7 emergency assistance

Safety During Safari

  • Always follow your guide's instructions
  • Never exit the vehicle unless your guide says it's safe
  • Keep noise levels low during game drives
  • Keep arms and legs inside the vehicle at all times
  • Don't approach or attempt to feed wildlife
  • At camps/lodges, follow designated paths and use escort services at night
  • Keep tent/room zipped/locked when not present
